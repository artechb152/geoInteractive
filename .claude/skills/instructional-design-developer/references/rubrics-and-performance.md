## choose the right rubric type

- **checklist**: best when steps are binary (done / not done) and order matters.
- **analytic rubric**: best when quality levels matter (communication, reasoning, craftsmanship).
- **holistic rubric**: best for quick overall judgments; less diagnostic.

default: analytic rubric for complex tasks; checklist for procedures/safety.

## analytic rubric template

| criterion | weight | level 4 (exceeds) | level 3 (meets) | level 2 (partial) | level 1 (insufficient) | common errors |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| correctness | 40% | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| process | 30% | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| communication | 30% | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

write level descriptors as **observable behaviors**.

## checklist template

| step | critical? (y/n) | observable indicator | pass/fail | notes |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | y | verifies __ before __ |  |  |

rule: if any **critical** step fails, overall = fail (unless user requests partial credit).

## scoring rules

common scoring options:
- weighted sum (analytic)
- must-pass critical steps (checklist)
- mastery threshold (e.g., 80% overall + no critical fails)

always state: total points, pass mark, and what happens on retake.

## rater guidance

include a short rater note section:

- what evidence to look for
- what not to penalize (accent, minor style differences)
- examples of borderline cases

## competency-based assessment (CBA)

use when the goal is to certify that a learner can perform a role, not just pass a test.

### CBA design principles

1. **define competencies**: each competency = knowledge + skills + behaviors needed for a role or task.
2. **map evidence sources**: for each competency, identify multiple evidence types (not just one test).
3. **set mastery thresholds**: learners must demonstrate competence, not just accumulate points.
4. **allow multiple attempts**: CBA focuses on achieving mastery, not ranking learners.

### CBA evidence framework

| competency | evidence type 1 | evidence type 2 | evidence type 3 | mastery threshold |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| troubleshooting | scenario quiz (≥80%) | live demonstration (rubric ≥ level 3) | supervisor observation (checklist pass) | 2 of 3 evidence types met |

### portfolio assessment

when the user asks for portfolio-based assessment:

- **define artifacts**: what work products should the learner collect? (reports, recordings, project outputs)
- **provide rubric per artifact**: use the analytic rubric template above.
- **reflection prompts**: ask learners to explain what they learned and how they would improve.
- **progression tracking**: show growth over time (early vs. late artifacts).

### CBA vs. traditional assessment

| aspect | traditional | competency-based |
|---|---|---|
| focus | knowledge recall | demonstrated performance |
| timing | fixed (end of course) | flexible (when ready) |
| scoring | points/percentages | mastery/not yet |
| failure | permanent grade | retry until mastery |
| evidence | single test | multiple sources |
